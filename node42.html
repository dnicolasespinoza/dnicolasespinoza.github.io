6.1 The wellbore environment

Wellbore stability is critical for drilling. A stable open-hole requires the surrounding sediment and rock to bear the stresses concentrated around the cavity. The surrounding rock must hold stresses until casing is set or for undetermined time if left uncased.

Wellbore stability depends on two set of variables: one set out of our control and another set that we can control.

  • In-situ variables out of our control include far-field stresses , pore presssure , and rock properties.
  • Controllable variables include mud pressure , mud composition, mud chemistry, and wellbore orientation (direction azimuth and deviation).
