Length scales v.s. process zone size

Petroleum and subsurface engineering involves rock failure at many length scales, from the millimeter-scale to the kilometer-scale (Figure 4.2). The failure properties of rock (and many other properties too) are length scale-dependent. Small-scale process zones engage the rock “matrix” properties. Rock cutting at the drill-bit scale and wellbore stability (in homogeneous and non-fractured rock) are two examples. The samples we test in the laboratory are at this small scale. Large-scale process zones involve fractures, multiple sedimentary layers, and faults. For example, hydraulic fracturing tends to reactivate neighboring fractures in shear and reservoir depletion can reactivate large faults. Recognizing the appropriate length-scale is extremely important to use adequately the rock strength measured in the laboratory and simple mechanical formulations such as linear elasticity.
